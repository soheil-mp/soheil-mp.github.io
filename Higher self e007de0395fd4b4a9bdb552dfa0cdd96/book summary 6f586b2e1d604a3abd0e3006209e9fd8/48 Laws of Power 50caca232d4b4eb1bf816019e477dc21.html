  1. Never outshine the master: This law suggests that you should never outdo your superiors, as it can make them feel threatened and undermine their authority. Instead, make them look better than you do, and give them credit for your successes. This law is especially important in hierarchical organizations where power dynamics are at play.

Friends can often let us down, while enemies can teach us valuable lessons and even push us to succeed. This law encourages us to be strategic and to use every situation to our advantage.
  1. Conceal your intentions: This law advises us to keep our plans secret and not reveal too much about our goals or strategies. By keeping our intentions hidden, we can avoid unwanted attention, protect ourselves from competition, and surprise our opponents when we make our moves. This law underscores the importance of discretion and strategic thinking.
  1. Always say less than necessary: This law suggests that we should speak less and listen more. It advises us not to reveal everything we know, as this can make us vulnerable and give others an advantage. Instead, we should be selective in what we say and use our words strategically to achieve our goals.

This law highlights the importance of integrity, accountability, and responsibility.
