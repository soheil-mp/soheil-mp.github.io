Without Expectation

Our practice is to sit to express our true nature, and not with the expectation of obtaining something. The same should be true with all other activity we do.

No Dualism

Dualism is a perception that something can be sorted into two fundamental and irreducible categories. There is no such thing as dualism in Zen. For example, good & evil, God & world, matter & spirit, body & mind.

Repetition Spirit

You need to integrate repetition without expectation inside yourself. That is to practice the same thing over and over every day until you become pretty good at it. Don’t loose the spirit of repetition.

Forget Excitement

It’s important to keep a constant way. Zen is not some kind of excitement, but a concentration in our usual everyday routine. If you become too excited and busy, you mind become rough and ragged which is not good. Try to make your mind calm, joyful, constant, stable.

Purify your Efforts

Purify your Efforts:

  • When practicing, you need to have a right effort in a right direction.
  • Keep your effort pure by avoid adding anything that is extra to it (e.g. expectation, attainment of something, etc.).
  • Our effort should derive from achievement to non achievement. That is because we want to get rid of the unnecessary and bad result of the effort. If you do something with the spirit of non achievement, there is a good quality in it.
  • Effort is not a dualistic activity.
  • When we do Zazen, we don’t want to gain anything. But, we want to purify ourself from impurity.

Detach your Mind

To keep your mind calm, you need to detach your mind from past, future, events, memories, and so on.

No Trace

No trace:

  • Trace is an idea or thinking that your mind is attached to it.
  • Creating trace in your mind makes your mind more complicated. A complicated mind do something in relation to many other things or people which is limiting.
  • A complex mind trys to catch two birds with one stone, which makes it very hard for them to concentrate on one activity and they might end up not catching any birds at all.
  • A relative mind makes decision in relative to other things (i.e. through traces) which limits itself.
  • Burn out all the thoughts that you have attached to it. So nothing remains except ashes. And an ash will never become fire, it always remain an ash.
  • You need to remember everything, but you need to burn down the attachments into them.
  • When practicing zazen, our mind is calm and pretty simple.

Giving

Giving:

  • Everything in origin is one, and when you give or create something, you will feel good because you will feel at one with what you’re giving.
  • Pāramitā is a Buddhist term often translated as "perfection". It is described in Buddhist commentaries as noble character qualities generally associated with enlightened beings.

    • Morality or buddhist precepts:

      • Avoid killing
      • Avoid stealing
      • Avoid sexual misconduct
      • Avoid lying
      • Avoid intoxicants
    • Endurance:

      The ability to endure an unpleasant or difficult process or situation without giving way.

    • Constant effort, energy
    • Zen practice and meditation

  • To give is non attachment. It doesn’t matter what you give. Even giving a penny or wisdom or building a bridge that people can use.

Limit your Activities

Limit your activity:

  • Limit your activity to what you can do just at this moment (to express your fully nature).
  • Just concentrate on the activity that you are doing at each moment. So:
    • When you bow, then you should just bow.
    • When you sit, then you should just sit.
    • When you eat then you should just eat.
  • When practicing Zazen, we are limiting our activity to smallest extend.

Common Mistakes

Common mistakes to avoid while practicing Zazen:

  • Do not have any idealistic or perfectionist expectation.
  • If your practice does not go well, do not get discourage. Find courage in discourage.
  • Keep the habit of repetition even if the practice does not go well.
  • Erase the competition mentality while practicing Zazen.
  • Practice without any expectation of joy.
  • While practicing, do not set any goal or purpose for it.
  • Don’t worship any particular thing.

Study Yourself

The purpose of Buddhism is to study ourself (and not Buddhism itself), and to study ourself, we need to forget about ourselves. When we forget ourselves, then we are the true activity of the big existence or the reality itself.

Approaching Problems

Approaching problem:

  • If you become you, whatever you do is Zazen.
  • You might often lose yourself (e.g. in zazen, you might get sleepy or your leg might feels painful).
  • When you loose yourself then your problems become problems. If you don’t loose yourself then your difficulties do not become a problem.
  • When you’re part of the problem or when problem is part of you then there is no problem. Because you’re the problem yourself. If so, then there is no problem.
  • If you start wonder about in some delusion which is something apart yourself then your surroundings isn’t real anymore which means your mind isn’t real any more. If you are deluded then your surroundings are delusion as well.
  • Once you’re in midst of delusion, then there is no end to the delusion. You’ll be in deluded ideas one after another.
  • Most people live in delusion involved in their problems and trying to solve their problems.
  • Just to live, is to live in problems. To solve the problem, is to be part of it.
  • If you become you, zazen becomes true zazen. So when you practice zazen, your problems (and everything else) will practice zazen.
  • Become one with problems means to solve the problems with single minded effort.
  • So if we eat the we must eat with our mind being present in there (and not wondering in some delusional ideas). This means you are you yourself.
  • When you are you, your seeing things as they are, and you become one with your surroundings. This is your true self.
